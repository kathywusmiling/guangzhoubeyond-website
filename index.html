Hidden Gems in Guangzhou

Places the guidebooks miss — personally visited and loved.

Cultural Icon

Chen Clan Ancestral Hall

A breathtaking example of traditional Cantonese architecture with intricate carvings, ceramics, and stonework. A true cultural must-see that most tourists walk right past.

✦ My top pick for first-timers
Hidden Escape

兰埔 (Lanpu)

A quiet, lush garden tucked away in the city — perfect for slowing down, reading, or simply breathing. A rare calm in a city of 16 million.

✦ Ideal for a Sunday afternoon
Trendy Neighbourhood

东山口 (Dongshankou)

A charming neighborhood of colonial-era architecture, independent cafes, boutiques, and wide tree-lined streets. Guangzhou's most photogenic corner.

✦ Great for a relaxed afternoon wander
Peaceful Temple

六榕寺 (Liurong Temple)

A peaceful Buddhist temple featuring a striking multi-story pagoda, far less crowded than the major tourist sites. Tranquil, historic, and beautiful.

✦ Less crowded than Guangxiao Temple
